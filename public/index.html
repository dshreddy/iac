About IAC

The Industrial Academia Conclave (IAC) at IIT Palakkad is a flagship event that bridges the gap between academic research and industrial application. As a vibrant platform where innovation meets expertise, our mission is to bridge the gap between industry and academia, fostering meaningful connections and progress.

At IAC, we believe in the power of collaboration to shape the future. Our events, including panel discussions, lecture series and talks by Industry leaders, and exhibitions, are designed to inspire, educate, and connect. From exploring cutting-edge technologies to addressing real-world challenges, our programs facilitate a rich exchange of ideas and perspectives.

IAC is where potential is realized, innovation knows no limits, and connections ignite fresh ideas. This is more than just a platform—it's a catalyst for change, where collective expertise drives impactful outcomes. Whether you're an industry professional, academic, or innovator, IAC offers the ideal environment to connect, learn, and grow.

Meet Our Chief Guest

Dr. Sreenivas Naidu

Dr. Sreenivas Naidu is a visionary leader in Research and Development, with a remarkable career spanning over two decades in mechanical engineering. His extensive experience includes pivotal roles in wind energy, rail, off-highway machinery, steel, and bearings industries. Renowned for his mechanical design and product development expertise, Dr. Naidu has driven innovation through finite element analysis and application engineering. His leadership in the Rail Application Engineering team at Timken Engineering and Research India has set a benchmark for excellence in supporting railway systems globally. Under his guidance, the Advanced Analysis team has excelled in delivering solutions for sectors such as wind energy, automotive, aerospace, and primary metals. Dr. Naidu's contributions include developing cutting-edge engineering tools and managing complex projects involving off-highway equipment and high-performance engines. Beyond his technical prowess, Dr. Naidu is known for his dedication to customer training, condition monitoring systems, and organizing impactful trade shows and seminars. His profound expertise and leadership continue to inspire innovation and set new standards across various engineering disciplines.

KeyNote Speakers

Dr. Sreedevi Upadhyayula

THERMOCHEMICAL WATER SPLITTING PROCESS DEVELOPMENT – A Case of Industry-Academia Nexus

In her talk, Dr. Upadhyayula will explore the promising potential of thermochemical cycles for water splitting as a sustainable method for large-scale hydrogen production. She will review various hydrogen production routes through thermochemical and hybrid cycles, discussing recent advances, major challenges, and future directions related to their design and scale-up. The focus will be on an efficient thermochemical process developed and demonstrated at IIT Delhi, supported by a long-term collaboration with the ONGC Energy Centre, showcasing a 15-year journey from ideation to technology demonstration.
Dr. Sreedevi Upadhyayula is a distinguished chemical engineer with extensive experience spanning both industry and academia. She earned her M.Tech in Chemical Engineering from IIT Kharagpur and began her career as a Senior Design Engineer in the public and private sectors. Following a PhD from the National Chemical Laboratory (NCL), Pune, and postdoctoral research at the University of Notre Dame, she joined IIT Kharagpur as an Assistant Professor. Currently, she holds the CLASS of 66 Chair Professor position at IIT Delhi.


Dr. Satya Chakravarthy

Dr. Satya Chakravarthy is a Professor of Aerospace Engineering at IIT Madras, where he has been a faculty member since 1997. He earned his B.Tech from IIT Madras and M.S. and Ph.D. from Georgia Tech, USA. His research focuses on propulsion and combustion in gas turbines, rocket engines, and electric aircraft. He leads the National Centre for Combustion Research & Development (NCCRD) at IIT Madras, the largest center of its kind globally, and the DRDO Centre of Propulsion Technology. He has received several prestigious awards, including the HAL Prize, the Young Engineer Award by the Indian National Academy of Engineering, and the DRDO Academic Excellence Award.

Beyond academia, Dr. Chakravarthy is the Founder and Technical Lead of The ePlane Company, which is developing hybrid aerial vehicles. He is also a director at Agnikul Cosmos and Aerostrovilos, focusing on space and power generation technologies, respectively. Additionally, he mentors Team Avishkar Hyperloop at IIT Madras and advises several tech startups, including NewTrace and GalaxEye. Dr. Chakravarthy is an accomplished speaker with TEDx talks and numerous online lectures, sharing his expertise in combustion and aerospace engineering with a global audience.


Mr. Shaik Aleem Ur Rehaman

Evolution of Modern SoC Architectures for AI/ML Workload and High Bandwidth Computations

In his upcoming talk, Mr. Aleem will delve into the latest trends in processors, memory, and fabrics, highlighting the essential skills needed for students and researchers aiming to excel in this domain. He will shed light on open-source tools and AI-based infrastructure for chip design and verification, as well as discuss government and industry initiatives in India that are driving semiconductor growth. The session will feature a fireside chat and Q&A, offering valuable insights and practical guidance for those interested in the semiconductor industry.

He is a distinguished ASIC Design and Verification Engineer with over 8 years of experience at the forefront of semiconductor technology. Currently at Microsoft, Aleem specializes in IP/Sub-System and SoC level design, with a focus on ultra-low-power dual-band radio chips and WiFi protocol verification. His expertise includes working with ARM Cortex processors, flash memory, and advanced verification methodologies.

Mr. Nahar Singh

AI Trends in Automotive Field

In his talk, Mr. Nahar will explore how AI is revolutionizing the automotive industry, making vehicles smarter, safer, and more efficient. He will discuss the latest trends in Generative AI (GenAI), highlighting advancements in vehicle design, prototyping, personalization, testing, simulation, and production optimization. Attendees can expect insights into current AI trends in the automotive field, as well as practical examples of how AI is reshaping the driving and ownership experience.
He is a seasoned professional and an alumnus of IIT Guwahati, currently advancing innovations at Continental. With extensive experience in autonomous driving, security, safety, and Industry 4.0, Nahar excels in integrating AI solutions across edge devices and cloud platforms. His expertise includes machine learning, image processing, and computer vision, driving successful projects with a focus on cutting-edge technology.

Lecture series and talks

DR. SANJAY KAMBLE

Case studies on process transformation from laboratory scale to commercial scale

CSIR-National Chemical Laboratory has strong capabilities in developing innovative processes and products for import substitution across various segments of the chemical industry. CSIR-NCL has developed innovative processes for import substitution in the chemical industry. Their work on flow reactors, continuous processes, and reactive distillation has improved performance, safety, and sustainability. This talk will present case studies for paracetamol, IBB, BPA, and plastic waste depolymerization.

DR. Siddhartha Gadgil

The future of Automated and Interactive Theorem Proving

Automated and Interactive Theorem proving is the discovery and verification of mathematical proofs with the help of computers. This has grown rapidly in the last few years and has been increasingly adopted in industry. Automated Theorem Proving very naturally complements Generative AI, which as we all know is powerful but unreliable. I will discuss the prospects of Automated Theorem Proving aiding and aided by generative AI, especially in Industry.

DR. SOURISH DAS

Doing Mathematics in the Era of Generative AI

In this talk, I will demonstrate how we can leverage generative AI tools, such as ChatGPT, to enhance our approach to mathematics. We will explore how to automate some of the mundane tasks in academic life using ChatGPT, thereby significantly boosting productivity for academicians. These techniques are also valuable for professionals in the corporate sector, particularly for those involved in coding, data analysis, and report writing. Finally, I will discuss the implications of Generative AI on our roles in society and what our place might be in this rapidly evolving landscape.

Dr. BROJESHWAR BHOWMICK

Clothing Dynamics on 3D people

This talk will present a novel approach to 3D human digitization and garment simulation. We will discuss how SMPL body parameterization can be used to capture human motion from monocular video and how this data can be leveraged to train a particle-based neural garment simulator. Our method overcomes limitations of existing approaches by learning the physical dynamics of garment vertices conditioned on underlying body shape, motion, and fabric properties, enabling generalization across various garment types, topologies, and fabric materials.

DR. Subho

Fostering Industry - Academia collaboration - A Pharmaceutical Perspective

Starting with a brief history of medicine the talk will walk you through various avenues of drug discovery and development, the Indian perspectives, requirements for knowledge sharing between industry and academia, bottlenecks and opportunities, role of a service provider in accelerating the process of discovery and development

Mr. Sudeep Chandrasekaran

Introduction to mmWave Radar

Software Engineer at Texas Instruments

Mr. N. Raghunath

Evolution of Mobile Networks towards Cloud RAN and ORAN - Opportunities and Challenges

The evolution of mobile networks from LTE to 5G has been driven by data consumption and technological advancements. While 3GPP standards focus on radio protocols, Cloud RAN and O-RAN aim to improve network efficiency and flexibility. Cloud RAN centralizes and virtualizes base station functions, while O-RAN promotes open interfaces and multi-vendor options. This evolution presents opportunities for innovation and new applications but also poses challenges for the technical community.

Mr ASHISH KUVELKAR

High Performance Computing - A Tool for Scientific Advancement

The talk will introduce the basics of High Performance Computing, how it serves the purpose of addressing the computational needs of scientific community, what are the methodologies used for making effective utilization of HPC, the HPC landscape in India and options available for the researchers for availing HPC services of National Supercomputing Mission.

Dr. KAUSHIK CHOUDHURY

From Concept to Creation: The Journey of a Product

We are living in an era of innovation. We are the generation that has transitioned from audio cassettes to CDs to DVDs to SD cards and then cloud music.Interestingly students pursuing research-oriented or research-driven courses are the best suited individuals for developing products, yet we do not see many of them doing that. This talk will be focusing on how research could be looked upon as a precursor to product development and entrepreneurship. It will also have some aspects about successful products to give an insight as to how creative thinking makes a product last longer than its expected lifetime.

Dr. KAVITA KADU

Revolutionizing Sustainability: Shaping the Future of Circular Economy and Climate Tech

APChemi stands at the forefront of combating two of the world's most pressing environmental challenges: plastic pollution and carbon emissions. As a leader in advanced recycling and carbon sequestration technologies, we offer innovative solutions that are transforming waste into valuable resources.

Dr. RAMASWAMY VISHNUDAS

External Gear Pump Technology

This talk delves into the latest R&D trends in External Gear Pumps, which are vital for mobile and industrial fluid power systems. We will explore innovations such as noise reduction, low-speed & efficiency optimization, advancements in testing and development. Additionally, the presentation will highlight our collaborative efforts with universities, particularly our Advanced Engineering department, which fosters industry-academic partnerships and student engagement through internships and joint research projects. It also discusses collaborations with universities and our approach to nurturing future talent.

MR. VISH RAJALINGAM

Behind the Software-Defined Vehicles: The Computational Needs of Modern Mobility

The automotive industry is undergoing a transformation driven by AI and ADAS. These innovations, coupled with the demand for advanced user experiences in infotainment, require significant computational power. GPUs, DSPs, and specialized AI Accelerators are ideal for handling the compute-intensive nature of modern mobility applications. This presentation will explore the AI workloads for ADAS and infotainment, discuss the advantages of GPU and DSP architectures, and demonstrate real-world use cases.

MR. AVINASH GADEKAR

Water Sector and opportunities

Associate director, AECOM India Global Services Pvt. Ltd.

DR. MILINDA MAHAJAN

Simplifying Complex Seismic Irregularities in Building Design

Seismically irregular buildings have significant challenges in structural analysis and design due to their complex geometry and non-uniform distribution of mass, stiffness, and strength. These irregularities can cause unpredictable seismic responses during an earthquake, and may risk safety and stability. The talk will focus on the aspects of the earthquake analysis of building structures and a case study to deal with seismically irregular building, its retrofitting strategies to make it simple regular building.

MR. ARJUN MENON

Building Core Generators for RISC-V

This talk will take you through a journey, which began with the fundamental motivation of constructing an open-source academic processor and evolved into developing highly configurable core generator architectures. Throughout the talk, we will explore technical and non-technical decisions that impact this ecosystem and the micro-architectural and scalability challenges that arise. Additionally, we will discuss some of the choices that were made to create a state-of-the-art embedded microprocessor portfolio with limited resources and a small team.

ADITHYA KUMAR O V

The Curious Case of Good Engineering

Senior software engineer, Composewell Technologies
