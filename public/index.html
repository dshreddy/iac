About IAC

The Industrial Academia Conclave (IAC) at IIT Palakkad is a flagship event that bridges the gap between academic research and industrial application. As a vibrant platform where innovation meets expertise, our mission is to bridge the gap between industry and academia, fostering meaningful connections and progress.

At IAC, we believe in the power of collaboration to shape the future. Our events, including panel discussions, lecture series and talks by Industry leaders, and exhibitions, are designed to inspire, educate, and connect. From exploring cutting-edge technologies to addressing real-world challenges, our programs facilitate a rich exchange of ideas and perspectives.

IAC is where potential is realized, innovation knows no limits, and connections ignite fresh ideas. This is more than just a platform—it's a catalyst for change, where collective expertise drives impactful outcomes. Whether you're an industry professional, academic, or innovator, IAC offers the ideal environment to connect, learn, and grow.

Chief Guest

Dr. Sreenivas Naidu

Dr. Sreenivas Naidu is an accomplished Research and Development professional with over 20 years of experience in mechanical engineering, specializing in industries such as wind energy, rail, off-highway, steel, and bearings. His expertise spans mechanical design, product development, finite element analysis, and application engineering, where he has successfully led global teams in delivering innovative solutions. Sreenivas has played a key role in managing the Rail Application Engineering team at Timken Engineering and Research India, Bangalore, ensuring the highest standards of support for railway customers worldwide. He has also overseen the Advanced Analysis team, catering to global clients in sectors like wind energy, automotive, aerospace, and primary metals. His strong background includes developing in-house engineering tools to meet global requirements and extensive hands-on experience with off-highway equipment and V-Type 12-cylinder engines for dump trucks. In addition to his technical acumen, Sreenivas excels in customer training, bearing condition monitoring systems, and organizing trade shows and seminars. His profound knowledge and leadership in mechanical engineering make him a distinguished figure in the field, consistently driving innovation and excellence across various industries.

KeyNote Speakers

Dr. Sreedevi Upadhyayula

Dr. Sreedevi Upadhyayula is a seasoned chemical engineer with a rich blend of industry and academic experience. After earning her M.Tech in Chemical Engineering from IIT Kharagpur in 1993, she worked as a Senior Design Engineer in both the public and private sectors. In 1998, she returned to academia as a CSIR Senior Research Fellow at the National Chemical Laboratory (NCL), Pune, and completed her PhD in 2001.

She then pursued postdoctoral research at the University of Notre Dame, USA, before starting her academic career as an Assistant Professor at IIT Kharagpur in 2004. Currently, she is the CLASS of 66 Chair Professor at IIT Delhi. Her research focuses on sustainable heterogeneous catalysis, zeotype materials in petrochemical and refinery catalytic processes, biomass conversion to biofuels, catalyst design, parametric study, kinetics and modeling of heterogeneous reactions.

Dr. Satya Chakravarthy

Dr. Satya Chakravarthy is a Professor of Aerospace Engineering at IIT Madras, where he has been a faculty member since 1997. He earned his B.Tech from IIT Madras and M.S. and Ph.D. from Georgia Tech, USA. His research focuses on propulsion and combustion in gas turbines, rocket engines, and electric aircraft. He leads the National Centre for Combustion Research & Development (NCCRD) at IIT Madras, the largest center of its kind globally, and the DRDO Centre of Propulsion Technology. He has received several prestigious awards, including the HAL Prize, the Young Engineer Award by the Indian National Academy of Engineering, and the DRDO Academic Excellence Award.

Beyond academia, Dr. Chakravarthy is the Founder and Technical Lead of The ePlane Company, which is developing hybrid aerial vehicles. He is also a director at Agnikul Cosmos and Aerostrovilos, focusing on space and power generation technologies, respectively. Additionally, he mentors Team Avishkar Hyperloop at IIT Madras and advises several tech startups, including NewTrace and GalaxEye. Dr. Chakravarthy is an accomplished speaker with TEDx talks and numerous online lectures, sharing his expertise in combustion and aerospace engineering with a global audience.

Mr. Shaik Aleem Ur Rehaman

Shaik Aleem Ur Rehaman is a seasoned ASIC Design and Verification Engineer with over 8 years of experience in IP/Sub-System and SoC level design, currently working at Microsoft. His expertise spans a wide range of cutting-edge technologies, including ultra-low-power dual-band radio chips, WiFi protocol verification, and full-chip hardware-software co-verification. Aleem has significant experience working with ARM Cortex dual-core M4 and M0+ processors, flash memory, and corresponding controllers. He has conducted OVM-based IP-level verification for eSPI controllers and worked on memory expanders for Xeon platforms using FPGA acceleration. Additionally, he has contributed to the Boeing Spacecraft Project, focusing on RS422 and RS485 feature verification.

Mr. Nahar Singh

Nahar Singh is an accomplished professional and an alumnus of IIT Guwahati, currently working at Continental. He has extensive expertise in areas such as autonomous driving, security, safety, Industry 4.0, video analytics, and cognitive data. Nahar has contributed to developing and deploying AI solutions both on edge devices and cloud platforms, providing technical guidance to his team while practicing leadership. His technical skills span machine learning, image processing, and computer vision, having successfully led projects in these domains. His work ethic, creative problem-solving abilities, and collaborative nature make him a valuable asset to any team. Additionally, Nahar's knowledge of AWS services for managing large data transactions, combined with his humor and positivity, contribute to his effectiveness in meeting tight deadlines. His rapid learning capability and dedication to research and development further enhance his role in transforming organizational visions into reality.

Lecture series and talks

DR. SANJAY KAMBLE

Dr. Sanjay P. Kamble is a researcher in the field of Chemical Engineering Science, working within the Chemical Engineering and Process Development Division. His research focuses on key areas such as Process Development and Scale-up, Process Intensification, and Photocatalysis for Energy and Environmental Applications. He is also involved in the defluoridation of water and the development of materials for the remediation of pollutants.

DR. Siddhartha Gadgil

Dr. Siddhartha Gadgil is a Professor of Mathematics at the Indian Institute of Science, Bangalore. After completing his Ph.D. at the California Institute of Technology, he joined IISc in 2006. His research spans Low-dimensional Topology, Geometric Group Theory, and automated theorem proving. Currently, he is focused on projects involving Lean Prover 4, including AI tools for proof discovery and theorem proving.

DR. SOURISH DAS

In this talk, I will demonstrate how we can leverage generative AI tools, such as ChatGPT, to enhance our approach to mathematics. We will explore how to automate some of the mundane tasks in academic life using ChatGPT, thereby significantly boosting productivity for academicians. These techniques are also valuable for professionals in the corporate sector, particularly for those involved in coding, data analysis, and report writing. Finally, I will discuss the implications of Generative AI on our roles in society and what our place might be in this rapidly evolving landscape.

Dr. BROJESHWAR BHOWMICK

This talk explores the intricate interplay between clothing dynamics and the movement of 3D human models. It delves into the fundamental principles of simulating realistic garment behavior, focusing on how fabric properties, body movements, and environmental factors influence the appearance and performance of clothing on virtual characters.

This talk delves into the latest R&D trends in External Gear Pumps, which are vital for mobile and industrial fluid power systems. We will explore innovations such as noise reduction, low-speed & efficiency optimization, advancements in testing and development. MR. VISH RAJALINGAM

VP and GM Mobility and transport business unit, Multicoreware The automotive industry is undergoing a transformation driven by AI and ADAS. These innovations, coupled with the demand for advanced user experiences in infotainment, require significant computational power. GPUs, DSPs, and specialized AI Accelerators are ideal for handling the compute-intensive nature of modern mobility applications. This presentation will explore the AI workloads for ADAS and infotainment, discuss the advantages of GPU and DSP architectures, and demonstrate real-world use cases. The presentation will showcase MulticoreWare's expertise in addressing the demanding challenges of modern mobility.

MR. AVINASH GADEKAR

Associate director, AECOM India Global Services Pvt. Ltd.

DR. MILINDA MAHAJAN

Seismically irregular buildings have significant challenges in structural analysis and design due to their complex geometry and non-uniform distribution of mass, stiffness, and strength. These irregularities can cause unpredictable seismic responses during an earthquake, and may risk the safety and stability. The talk will focus on the aspects of the earthquake analysis of building structures and a case study to deal with seismically irregular building, its retrofitting strategies to make it simple regular building.

MR. ARJUN MENON

Co Founder and chief engineer, Incore semiconductors Pvt. Ltd. This talk will take you through a journey, which began with the fundamental motivation of constructing an open-source academic processor and evolved into developing highly configurable core generator architectures. Throughout the talk, we will explore technical and non-technical decisions that impact this ecosystem and the micro-architectural and scalability challenges that arise. Additionally, we will discuss some of the choices that were made to create a state-of-the-art embedded microprocessor portfolio with limited resources and a small team. Speaker Bio: Arjun Menon is the Co-founder and Chief Engineer of InCore Semiconductors. He was one of the key architects of the SHAKTI processor program at IIT Madras. He was also the lead architect of India’s first indigenous microprocessor, which was designed and fabricated in India. While at IIT Madras, he spearheaded the development of a 64-bit Out-of-Order processor based on a custom instruction set for DRDO, and, a custom SoC for ISRO Inertial Systems Unit. His primary areas of interest include computer architecture and secure microprocessor design.
